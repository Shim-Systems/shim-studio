shim / ʃɪm /

1. A thin piece of material used to align physical parts, make them fit, or reduce friction.

// THE HAAS EDGE

A physical edge checkpoint engineered directly for on-site operations.

Our Hardware-as-a-Service (HaaS) model deploys a rugged, plug-and-play edge computer (the SHIM) directly in your site offices. By capturing audit-ready data right at the physical boundary, the SHIM appliance bypasses complex IT integrations, respects subcontractor business secrets, and operates reliably in the most demanding site environments.

// FEATURE_01

Bypasses the IT Gatekeeper Wall

Our physical edge appliance operates as an outbound-only device, running independently of internal corporate infrastructure via local connectivity or autonomous cellular fallbacks. It requires zero corporate IT integration, zero firewall exceptions, and zero network modifications to work immediately.
// FEATURE_02

Protects Subcontractor Pricing

The edge appliance extracts strictly physical metrics—like material volumes, aggregate grades, and transport runs—from delivery dockets, keeping confidential business pricing completely secure and off the cloud.
// FEATURE_03

Collaborative Adoption: Fast-Track Payments

Capturing and pre-verifying dockets instantly at the gatehouse checkpoint eliminates end-of-month administrative matching disputes, allowing subcontractors to get progress claims approved and paid faster.
// FEATURE_04

Circular Economy & "Recycled First" Auditing

Physical dockets are captured at the gate to automatically extract, verify, and compile recycled material volumes, generating audit-ready compliance reports without administrative double-handling.
// FEATURE_05

Engineered for the Chaos of the Site Compound

Built for heavy construction environments, our fanless, dustproof industrial hardware features built-in electrical over-protection and a supercapacitor backup to ensure clean local database saves during sudden power cuts.
// FEATURE_06

Paid from Project "Preliminaries" (OpEx over CapEx)

By bundling the physical node, local database, and extraction software into a flat-rate monthly lease, the utility is funded directly out of local project preliminaries, bypassing corporate purchasing delays.
